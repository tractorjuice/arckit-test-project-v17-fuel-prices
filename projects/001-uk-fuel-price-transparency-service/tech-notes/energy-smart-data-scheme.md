# Energy Smart Data Scheme

## Overview

The Energy Smart Data Scheme is a DESNZ/DSIT policy programme that will enable consumers to securely share their energy data (e.g., consumption patterns) with authorised third parties to receive personalised services — price comparison, supplier switching, and low-carbon tariff recommendations.

## Key Facts

- **Led by**: DESNZ (policy) and DSIT (digital/data)
- **Legal basis**: Smart Data provisions in the Data Protection and Digital Information Act
- **Target MVP**: Late 2026
- **Regulations**: Consultation planned 2026; regulations targeted 2027/28
- **Scope**: Electricity and gas markets initially

## Relationship to Fuel Finder

The Fuel Finder is effectively an early exemplar of a government smart data scheme, though implemented through sector-specific regulations (Motor Fuel Price (Open Data) Regulations 2025) rather than the Smart Data framework. Key parallels:

| Aspect | Fuel Finder | Energy Smart Data |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Data type** | Fuel prices per forecourt | Energy consumption per consumer |
| **Sharing model** | Open data (publish to all) | Consent-based (consumer-directed) |
| **Regulations** | Motor Fuel Price Regulations 2025 | Smart Data provisions (TBC 2027/28) |
| **Platform operator** | VE3 Global (DESNZ contract) | TBC |
| **Regulator** | CMA | Ofgem |
| **Third-party access** | Open API (anyone) | Authorised Third Parties only |

## Strategic Implications

1. **Shared infrastructure**: Both schemes need API platforms, data quality frameworks, compliance monitoring, and third-party ecosystems. Shared components could reduce costs.
2. **Policy precedent**: Fuel Finder's operational experience (retailer compliance rates, data quality challenges, third-party adoption) provides evidence for Energy Smart Data policy design.
3. **Cross-scheme efficiencies**: Identity verification (GOV.UK One Login), notification services (GOV.UK Notify), and compliance monitoring patterns could be reused.

## Smart Data Strategy (April 2026)

The Government published its Smart Data Strategy in April 2026, setting out how smart data schemes across sectors (energy, fuel, finance via Open Banking) will be coordinated. This creates opportunities for Fuel Finder to be positioned as a pathfinder.
