## Summary

GOV.UK Forms is a free government platform for creating accessible online forms, operated by the Government Digital Service (GDS). It is used by 80+ government organisations with 420+ forms (live and archived) and 560,947 submissions since September 2022 (as of March 2026). It is relevant to the Fuel Finder project as a potential platform for building a CMA-specific error reporting form (Phase 2 of Category 12: Citizen Error Reporting).

## Key Findings

### Platform Statistics (as of March 2026)
- 80+ government organisations using the platform
- 420+ forms (live and archived)
- 560,947+ form submissions since September 2022
- During trial phase: 1,200 civil servants adopted the tool
- Government reports it has saved > 2 years in processing time

### Features
- Accessible GOV.UK templates meeting public sector accessibility requirements
- Automatic form response validation (missing answers, wrong format)
- Simple metrics (submission count, completion rate)
- Meets latest government cyber security standards
- GDPR-compliant data protection
- Free for central government departments

### Technical Details
- URL: forms.service.gov.uk
- Operator: Government Digital Service (GDS)
- Cost: Free for central government
- Integration: Form responses delivered to specified email address or API endpoint

### Relevance to Fuel Finder Project
- **Phase 2 Error Reporting**: If citizen feedback volume exceeds 100 reports/month, GOV.UK Forms could be used to build a CMA-specific error reporting form that feeds into the compliance dashboard
- **Advantages over custom build**: Zero cost, already accessible, GDS-maintained, consistent with GOV.UK design patterns
- **Limitations**: May not support complex workflows (e.g., auto-linking reports to specific forecourt IDs in CMA database)
